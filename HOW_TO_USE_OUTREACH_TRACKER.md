# How to Use the Outreach Tracker

The outreach tracker is a simple workbook for managing fundraising, partnership, and customer outreach in one place.

## What to Enter

Each row should represent one contact or one organization you plan to reach.

- `Name`
  The main person you are contacting
- `Organization`
  The foundation, company, nonprofit, or partner name
- `Email`
  The best contact email
- `Status`
  Current stage such as `Not Contacted`, `Contacted`, `Follow-up Sent`, `Meeting Scheduled`, `Proposal Sent`, `Won`, or `Closed / No Response`
- `Follow-up Date`
  The next date you want to follow up
- `Last Contact`
  The last date you emailed or spoke with them
- `Notes`
  Anything useful: grant deadlines, referral source, meeting details, or special interests

## Recommended Workflow

1. Add your top outreach targets first.
2. Set `Status` to `Not Contacted` until the first email goes out.
3. After sending the first message, update:
   - `Status` to `Contacted`
   - `Last Contact` to today’s date
   - `Follow-up Date` to about two weeks later
4. If you send a follow-up, update the status again and move the next follow-up date forward.
5. Record all important notes immediately after calls or emails.

## Best Practices

- Review the tracker at least twice per week.
- Sort or scan by `Follow-up Date` so nothing slips.
- Keep notes short and practical.
- Use one row per real decision-maker whenever possible.
- Mark cold leads clearly so you focus on the strongest opportunities first.

## Goal

The tracker should help you answer three questions quickly:

1. Who have I contacted?
2. Who needs a follow-up next?
3. Which leads are closest to funding, partnership, or deployment?
